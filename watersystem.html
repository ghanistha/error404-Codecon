Global Water Availability

Tracking freshwater resources and their distribution.

Freshwater Reserves

Only 2.5% of the Earth's water is freshwater, and less than 1% is accessible for human use.

Rainfall Patterns

Climate change is altering rainfall patterns, leading to droughts in some regions and floods in others.

Glacial Melt

Glaciers are melting at an alarming rate, threatening water supplies for millions of people.

Global Water Quality

Assessing the health of our water sources.

Pollution Levels

Industrial and agricultural runoff contaminate water sources with chemicals and pollutants.

Bacterial Contamination

Lack of sanitation leads to widespread bacterial contamination, causing waterborne diseases.

Salinity Intrusion

Rising sea levels cause salinity intrusion into freshwater sources, making them unusable.

Human Impacts on Water Systems

Understanding the consequences of our actions on water resources.

Public Health

Water scarcity and contamination lead to increased rates of waterborne diseases and malnutrition.

Food Security

Water shortages threaten agricultural production, leading to food insecurity and famine.

Displacement

Water scarcity and flooding force communities to migrate, leading to displacement and conflict.
